    Module One Oral language development

    Read

    • practice piece: https://www.kqed.org/mindshift/40040/the-benefits-of-helping-preschoolers-understand-and-discuss-their-emotions
    • Research piece: Teacher–Child Conversation in the Preschool Classroom

    Write

    • What can teachers do to increase oral language development in pre-school

    Participate

    • Watch X video from Atlas. Evaluate the dialogue in the classroom. How did the teacher invite students to take intellectual risk.

    Module Two Emergent Literacy

    Read:

    • practice piece: http://www.readingrockets.org/article/literacy-rich-environments
    • research piece: Enhancing Phonological Awareness, Print Awareness, and Oral Language Skills in Preschool Children

    Write

    • How will you support emergent literacy in your classroom?

    Participate

    • With a 1-2 other students in class design what you think would be the perfect preschool schedule

    Module Three Concepts About Print

    Read

    • practice piece: Marie Clay
    • research piece: Toddlers, touchscreens, and tablet technologies: Learning “concepts beyond print”

    Write

    • How should we teach Concepts of Print

    Participate

    • Record a Concepts of Print Selfie

    Module Four Stages of Development

    Read

    http://newlearningonline.com/literacies/chapter-15/chall-on-stages-of-reading-development

    Write

    • Describe a student at each stage of reading development. What can they do? what would you do as a teacher?

    Participate

    • Make a stages of reading poster, pic, or slidedeck and share with the class

    Module Five Phonemic Awareness

    Read

    • Practioner Piece: http://www.readingrockets.org/sites/default/files/Building%20the%20Foundation.pdf
    • research piece: http://www.jstor.org/stable/pdf/748111.pdf

    Write

    • Find your five favorite example of phonemic teaching on YouTube and share the videos on your blog. Use evidence from the reading to support your choices.

    Participate.

    • Develop and share a plan to teach phonemic awareness. Remember its 7 mintues a day and not a lesson plan. What are all the fun ways you will play with sounds.

    Module Six Phonics

    Read

    https://my.vanderbilt.edu/specialeducationinduction/files/2011/09/1-Literacy-teaching-guide-phonics.pdf

    Write

    Participate

    We will be meeting f2f on June 12. You will be in small groups and will live teach a phonics lesson
